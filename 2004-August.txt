documentation, and programing with expat, libxml, or sax looks fairly
complicated.

pkgbuild now has to simply be imported.  Personally I love the idea
because it removes the need for *alot* of code. (to open the file, to
parse it, to put the parsed data into some sort of structure, etc).

What do you think Seth? Yah or ney?

It's not as simple as you think... just look at the way portage,
pacman, etc do it.  It seems there is no way to do it without using a
complex algorithm.  When I said it was hard that was an
understatement, I been working on the same damn function for the past
two days!  To be honest... I'm now *pissed*.

Right of the bat I like the idea of using arch packages, but before
just shooting down the idea or just going with it I think we should
look and see what exactly an arch package is, what creating one
entails, and how they work.

If everything seems to meet up to our standards then I see absolutely
why we shouldn't use it.  If the arch packages have most of what we
need there's reason why we couldn't add a few, optional "enhancements"
thus making arch packages a subset of spiffy packages.

So it appears we have three options now:
1) use only arch packages
2) use mixed arch packages and superarch packages (spiffy "enhanced").
3) keep on doing what we are doing.

I personally think that we need to look more into arch packages maybe
read up of how they are made, then make some sort of decision.  (even
though I'm already kinda leaning towards #2 because it allows us to
use a huge amount of already available packages, and it also allows us
to use special features that make spiffy unique.
